01About this policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Paddock Route Pty Ltd (ABN 73 512 858 386) (Paddock Route, we, us, our) collects, uses, stores and discloses personal information about you.

We are bound by the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This Policy applies to information we collect through our website at paddockroute.com.au, our subscription service, our social media accounts, and any other interactions you have with us.

By using the Service, you agree to your personal information being handled as described in this Policy.

02Information we collect

The types of personal information we collect depend on how you interact with us. They may include:

Account and contact details

  • your name, email address, phone number and delivery address;
  • account login credentials (passwords are stored as one-way hashes); and
  • any preferences or notes you provide (such as dietary preferences, allergies, or delivery instructions).

Order and payment details

  • your order history, Box preferences, and substitution choices;
  • billing address and the last four digits of your payment card (full card details are held by our payment processor, not us); and
  • delivery confirmations, photos taken at the point of delivery, and any feedback you submit.

Communications

  • messages you send us by email, social media, or through our contact forms; and
  • records of phone calls or other interactions with our team.

Technical information

  • your IP address, device type, browser, operating system, and approximate location;
  • pages you visit, time spent on the Site, and the referring page or campaign; and
  • information collected via cookies and similar technologies (see section 07).

We don't generally collect "sensitive information" as defined in the Privacy Act (such as health information). If you choose to tell us about an allergy or dietary requirement, we'll only use that information to help fulfil your order and to keep you safe.

03How we collect it

We collect personal information in several ways:

  • Directly from you — when you create an account, place an order, contact us, subscribe to our newsletter, or fill in a form on our Site.
  • Automatically — through cookies, server logs and analytics tools when you use the Site.
  • From third parties — such as our payment processor (confirming a successful charge), delivery partners (confirming a delivery), or social media platforms when you interact with our pages.

If you provide personal information about someone else (for example, when ordering a gift Box), you must have their consent and let them know about this Policy.

04How we use your information

We use your personal information to:

  • process your orders, deliver your Boxes, and manage your Subscription;
  • communicate with you about your account, orders, and any issues with delivery or payment;
  • provide customer support and respond to your queries;
  • send you marketing communications about products, offers and news (you can opt out at any time — see section 08);
  • personalise your experience and improve the Service;
  • analyse how customers use the Site and measure the performance of marketing campaigns;
  • prevent fraud, enforce our Terms, and protect our rights and the rights of others; and
  • comply with our legal obligations (for example, tax records).

We won't use your personal information for any purpose that you wouldn't reasonably expect, unless you've consented or we're required by law.

05Who we share it with

We share personal information with third parties only where it's necessary to provide the Service or required by law. These categories include:

  • Payment processors — to take payments and detect fraud (currently Stripe Payments Australia Pty Ltd);
  • Delivery partners and drivers — to deliver your Box (your name, address, phone number and any delivery notes are shared with the driver assigned to your run);
  • Email and SMS providers — to send transactional and marketing messages (for example, order confirmations and weekly Box previews);
  • Analytics and advertising platforms — such as Google Analytics and Meta (Facebook/Instagram) pixels, in de-identified or aggregated form where possible;
  • Cloud hosting providers — who store our data on our behalf under contractual obligations of confidentiality;
  • Professional advisers — such as accountants and lawyers, where we need their advice; and
  • Government bodies or courts — where required by law (such as a subpoena, court order, or legitimate request from a law enforcement agency).

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

06Payment information

Payments are processed by our PCI-DSS compliant payment provider. We do not store or have access to your full card number, expiry, or CVV. We only retain a token reference and the last four digits, which we use to identify your card in your account and on receipts.

Our payment provider has its own privacy policy which we recommend you review.

07Cookies and analytics

We use cookies and similar technologies to make the Site work, to remember your preferences, and to understand how the Site is used. The main types we use are:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — required for the Site to function (such as keeping you logged in or remembering items in your cart);
  • Analytics cookies — to measure traffic and usage (such as Google Analytics); and
  • Marketing cookies — to measure the performance of campaigns and serve relevant ads (such as the Meta pixel).

You can control or block cookies through your browser settings. Blocking cookies may affect how parts of the Site work.

08Direct marketing

If you've signed up to our newsletter or are an active subscriber, we may send you marketing communications about new Boxes, seasonal produce, grower stories, or special offers. We'll always include a way to unsubscribe in every marketing email.

To stop receiving marketing communications, click the unsubscribe link at the bottom of any email, or email us at info@paddockroute.com.au. We may still send you transactional messages about your account or orders.

09Overseas disclosure

Some of our service providers (for example, cloud hosting and analytics) are based overseas or may store data on servers outside Australia. This typically includes the United States and the European Union.

Where we disclose personal information overseas, we take reasonable steps to ensure that the recipient handles the information consistently with the APPs, including by entering into appropriate contractual protections.

10Storage and security

We store personal information on secure servers operated by reputable cloud providers. We use industry-standard measures to protect your information, including:

  • encryption of data in transit (HTTPS/TLS);
  • access controls and authentication for our team members;
  • regular security reviews of our systems and processes; and
  • contractual security obligations on our third-party providers.

No system is perfectly secure. We can't guarantee absolute security, but we take privacy and security seriously and notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) if a notifiable data breach occurs.

We keep personal information only for as long as we need it for the purposes described in this Policy or as required by law (for example, tax law generally requires us to keep transaction records for at least seven years). When we no longer need information, we delete or de-identify it.

11Accessing and correcting your information

You have the right under the Privacy Act to:

  • request access to the personal information we hold about you; and
  • request that we correct any information that is inaccurate, incomplete or out of date.

You can update most account information directly through your account dashboard. For other requests, contact us using the details in section 15. We'll respond within a reasonable time, usually within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before releasing information.

12Complaints

If you believe we've handled your personal information in breach of the Privacy Act or this Policy, please contact us first at info@paddockroute.com.au. We'll acknowledge your complaint within 7 days and aim to resolve it within 30 days.

If you're not satisfied with our response, you can lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):

Website: www.oaic.gov.au
Phone: 1300 363 992
Post: GPO Box 5288, Sydney NSW 2001

13Children

The Service is not intended for, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from, anyone under 18. If you are under 18, please don't provide us with personal information. If you believe we've inadvertently collected information from a minor, please contact us so we can delete it.

14Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Where changes are material, we'll notify you by email or a prominent notice on the Site before they take effect.
